## Design Context

### Users
**Primary persona**: C-Suite executives (CEOs, COOs, General Directors) at mid-to-large Latin American organizations. They are time-poor, skeptical of consultants, and have been burned by change initiatives before. They visit from desktop, usually after a referral or search, and make decisions based on perceived credibility and differentiation — not features. Their job: find a transformation partner they can trust to not disrupt their organization into chaos.

**Market**: Latin America (primarily Mexico, Colombia, Argentina, Chile). Spanish-language first. Content tone should reflect formal yet accessible business Spanish.

**Use case**: Evaluate whether Henka is the right partner for an organizational transformation, digital change, or leadership development engagement. Primary conversion goal: book a free consultation ("Consulta Gratuita").

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Authoritative. Energizing. Human.

**Tagline**: "Hacemos del cambio un lugar seguro" — We make change a safe place.

**Voice**: Confident without arrogance. Warm without being casual. Uses the language of strategy (transformation, impact, change) but avoids management consulting jargon. Speaks *to* executives, not *at* them.

**Emotional goal**: Within 5 seconds, a visitor should feel *inspired and energized* — "Change is possible and exciting. I want that for us." This is not a fear-of-missing-out play; it is aspirational energy.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Primary reference**: McKinsey-style authority — high editorial quality, generous white space, data-informed credibility, premium typography, and controlled use of imagery. Think: confident restraint.

**Current implemented direction**: Swiss/Blueprint technical aesthetic — navy (#0c344d) + action green (#84bd2a), 0px border radius throughout, Open Sans typography, 1px structural grid lines at 10-15% opacity, left-aligned content hierarchy.

**Anti-references**:
- Generic SaaS startup (Notion/Linear clones — too cold, product-led)
- AI slop aesthetic (cyan-on-dark, purple gradients, glassmorphism, gradient text on metrics)
- Overly warm/boutique (photography-heavy, pastel, feels like therapy not strategy)

**Theme**: Light mode primary. Dark navy sections used for high-impact moments (hero, CTA).

**Brand colors**:
- Authority navy: #0c344d
- Action green: #84bd2a
- Light surface: #f4f6f9
- Accent text green: #82bd23

### Design Principles

1. **Authority through restraint** — Every element earns its place. No decorative noise. The grid, the whitespace, and the typography *are* the design. Inspired by McKinsey's editorial discipline.

2. **Energize, don't alarm** — Change management clients are often anxious. The visual language should project competence and momentum, not urgency or fear. Green for action, navy for trust.

3. **Executive-grade clarity** — C-suite visitors scan, they don't read. Visual hierarchy must communicate the value proposition in 5 seconds. One primary action visible at all times.

4. **LATAM professional register** — Content and tone should reflect formal Latin American business culture: direct, respectful, professionally warm. Not cold corporate English-translated-to-Spanish.

5. **Blueprint precision** — The 1px structural grid system is a deliberate brand metaphor: change is engineered, not improvised. Sharp corners (0px radius), precise spacing (8pt grid), and structural lines reinforce methodological rigor.
